There’s a lot of gear available to home sewers, ask me how I know, but to get started, you don’t need much at all.

- **Needles**.  You can get a pack that are different lengths and sharpness and they will cover almost every single scenario.  I still use my pack that I bought 15 years ago
- **Thread**.  I usually recommend both black and white, but take a look at your wardrobe and see if there’s any colours you wear a lot (I also always keep pink and navy on hand).  Avoid the clear thread, it’s difficult to use and will just make you mad
- **Scissors**.  If you’re just working with thread, any scissors will do, but if you’re going to be cutting fabric, fabric scissors will feel a lot nicer.  Tie a ribbon on them and don’t store them in your kitchen, they should only cut fabric or else they will become blunt
- **A ruler**.  I know tape measures are associated with sewing, and they are useful, but I have a 12” x 2” clear gridded ruler that I use all the time and can’t live without
- Technically optional, but highly recommended, **an iron and ironing board**.  Everytime you stitch something, it will benefit from being pressed, as it helps set the stitches (no, a steamer won’t cut it).  Ironing your clothes is also a very fast and easy way to look really sharp

**Bonus**, find yourself a cute container to put your sewing kit in.  A cookie tin is classic, but old shoeboxes, vases and those zippered bags you get from the dentist all work.  I keep my tools in souvenir cups from Citi Field.  They look like bouquets of scissors and rulers!
