<img src="/assets/wineglass.svg" alt="A glass of wine on a Saturday afternoon" style="float: left; shape-outside: url(/assets/wineglass.svg); width: 15%;">Okay so I don't barter all the time.  I had told Michelle I would teach her how to fix the hem on her trousers, they were the correct length it's just the stitches had broken.  However, they were black trousers, we'd had a glass of wine, and I said, look, this is going to take *no time at all* on the sewing machine, so let's do that.

(Actually now that I think about it, I think she brought the wine.  She definitely brought the good hummus.)

<aside class="font-dm-sans">
    The stitch I intended to teach Michelle is the <a href="https://grainlinestudio.com/blogs/blog/how-to-sew-a-slip-stitch" target="_blank">slip stitch</a>, a fantastic one to know if you're just tidying up hems.  Another way to fix Michelle's trousers would've been to use <a href="https://www.wawak.com/garment-construction/tapes/stitch-witchery-fusible-bonding-web/#sku=hmf101" target="_blank">Stitch Witchery</a>, which doesn't require any sewing but does require an iron.
</aside>
