To get started, you don’t need much at all.

- **Needles**.  You can get a pack that are different lengths and sharpness for almost every single scenario
- **Thread**.  Get colours that roughly match your clothes.  Avoid the clear thread, it’s difficult to use and will just make you mad
- **Scissors**.  If you’re just working with thread, any scissors will do, but if you’re going to be cutting fabric, fabric scissors will feel a lot nicer
- **A ruler**.  While tape measures are useful, I have a 12” x 2” clear gridded ruler that I use all the time and can’t live without
- Technically optional, but highly recommended, **an iron and ironing board**.  Everytime you stitch something, it will benefit from being pressed (no, a steamer won’t cut it).  Ironing your clothes is also a very fast and easy way to look really sharp

**Bonus**, find yourself a cute container to put your sewing kit in.  A cookie tin an old shoebox, vases, those zippered bags you get from the dentist, they all work.  I keep my tools in souvenir cups from Citi Field.
