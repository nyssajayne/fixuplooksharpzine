I actually did this alteration last year, but it's such a common thing that people tell me about I wanted to share how I fix it.

It's very common, *so common*, to find that the inner thigh on a pair of pants or jeans wears a hole.  This is not the fault of anyone's body, this is the fault of the garment.  The way the fabric is cut to shape the body means that it is prone to stretching, and when something is stretched, it will wear out faster.  Combine that with a declining quality in fabrics, and no wonder pants fail us sometimes.

The fix requires patching the hole.  By the time there is a hole, the mend will technically be visible, but if someone is staring at your crotch there is a different conversation you are likely having.  For Kristina's black polycotton trousers, I used some scrap black fabric I had.

<aside>
    <p>The trick is to fix it <em>before</em> there is a hole.  There's an old tailoring technique I read about (Classic Tailoring Techniques by Cabrera and Myers) where the crotch is reinforced with lining fabric.  There is still friction while you walk, but the lining takes the brunt of the heat.  When it starts to wear, you can replace the patch.</p>
</aside>
