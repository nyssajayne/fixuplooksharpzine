I love to buy supplies in person – it gives you a chance to actually touch things, which I feel is important when making and altering clothes, plus you can usually just buy what you need, rather than meeting a minimum order required on many websites.

If you’re in New York, the Garment District is just south of Times Square in Manhattan.  While not as bustling as it once was, it’s still a great resource and primarily made up of independent businesses.  My top shops include:

1. SIL Thread.  My favourite place for thread and tools, plus they have a wide array of high quality zippers.  You could get most of your sewing kit right here.
2. Pacific Trimming.  This is a great one-stop for buttons, trim, Rit Dye, embroidery floss, basically any little thing you might need.  There are “rules” to follow while shopping – make sure to get your buttons bagged before going to the counter and staff will help you cut any trims
3. Steinlof and Stoller.  I really love their button selection here, it’s much smaller than anywhere else, but they have a lot of vintage deadstock here, so you might be able to find something quite special
4. Mood Fabrics.  Mood is the closest thing to an all-in-one fabric and haberdashery, so if you were good at navigating Joanns, you’ll be able to handle Mood.  They stock pretty much everything, but I find it’s often quite expensive and busy

Getting to the Garment District can be tough, as most stores close by 6pm and keep short hours on Saturday, if they’re open at all.  If you need to order online, Wawak is an industry favourite.  They have great prices and if you order over $35, express shipping is the default.
