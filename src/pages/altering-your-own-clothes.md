Altering your clothes can be so much more than just mending.  To me, alterations not only allow you to breathe new life into old styles, but it’s also a chance to make your clothes tell your story.  My favourite and easy ways of altering clothes include

1. Cut it.  This works best on knit fabrics, like t-shirts and athleisure wear.  Cut the sleeves off t-shirts, make cropped sweaters, cut patterns into gym shorts.  The results will look imperfect, but that’s a part of the charm
2. Change the buttons.  Different buttons can make an inexpensive shirt look luxe, or a plain shirt more exciting
3. Add trim.  I’m a sucker for some lace on a neckline, but I also like adding ribbon to trousers to emulate a tuxedo stripe or a contrasting trim along the bottom of a skirt.  Avoid craft stores for supplies – garment quality trim will move with your clothes and look a lot more intentional
4. Dye it.  Rit dye comes in dozens of colours and their website has recipes to make many more.  Dyeing clothes not only means you get a fresh colour to wear, but it can also hide any tough stains or general shabbiness in a well loved garment.  My hot tips include making sure to read the instructions on the bottle and check the label inside your garment to prepare the right recipe, make sure to keep stirring, and keep an open mind about the results
5. Experiment with embroidery.  This can be as easy as adding patches, but if you’re feeling super crafty and have some patience, grab some embroidery floss and follow a youtube tutorial
