Travis has long been a supporter of the clothes that I make and he’s always made an effort to introduce me to other creatives.  He asked if I could replace the broken drawstring in his trousers, his John Varvatos trousers.  Travis offered me money, but instead, I asked for an IOU.  Travis is a very good gardener who grows his own vegetables, and I knew he’d have tomatoes at the height of summer.  It was a deal.

I took the drawstring out of the trousers and was able to find an exact match for the twill tape and a very similar match for the tips on the end.  However, I could not get the tips to look neat on the end of the twill tape.  Normally I am a perfection-is-the-enemy-of-completion type when it comes to sewing and alterations, but these weren’t meeting my expectations.  I was thinking of going back to the store to get more tips so I could keep practicing.

While I was completing this task, Travis mentioned that he found an old drawstring from something else and it fit around his waist, maybe we could use that?  I brought the trousers back to him at work, and while he tended bar, I threaded the new drawstring through.  It looked perfect, and Travis was so excited, which was such a reward.
